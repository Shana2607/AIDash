Green Campus Project

Eco-Friendly DIY Projects

Engage students in hands-on activities where they create eco-friendly products or solutions. For instance, they can make their own natural cleaning products, reusable shopping bags, or upcycle old materials into new items. This promotes creativity, resourcefulness, and a deeper understanding of sustainable alternatives.

Sustainable Food Choices

Organize a session where students learn about the environmental impact of food choices. Encourage discussions on topics such as plant-based diets, local and seasonal food, reducing food waste, and supporting sustainable agriculture. Students can even participate in a cooking workshop using sustainable ingredients and share their recipes.

Waste Audit and Recycling Campaign

Conduct a waste audit in the school or classroom to assess the types and amounts of waste generated. Students can analyze the findings and develop strategies to reduce waste, promote recycling, or implement composting. They can then design and launch a recycling campaign to raise awareness and encourage proper waste management practices.

Sustainable Fashion Show

Organize a fashion show where students showcase outfits made from sustainable materials, second-hand clothing, or upcycled garments. This activity promotes creativity, critical thinking about fashion industry practices, and the importance of conscious consumption.

Environmental Art Project

Encourage students to express their understanding of sustainability through art. They can create artwork using recycled materials, depict environmental themes, or design posters and murals to raise awareness about sustainability. This activity combines creativity, self-expression, and environmental advocacy.

Sustainable Gardening

Establish a school or classroom garden where students learn about organic gardening practices, composting, and the importance of biodiversity. Students can be involved in planning, planting, and maintaining the garden, fostering a sense of responsibility and connection to nature.
